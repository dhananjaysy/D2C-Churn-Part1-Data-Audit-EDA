# Business Memo: Customer Churn Analysis Findings

### To: 
Product, Marketing and Customer Support Teams

### From: 
Data Analytics Team

### Subject: 
Key Findings from Customer Churn Analysis

---

### Executive Summary

A review of customer transactions, support tickets, website activity and campaign history was conducted to better understand customer churn. The analysis identified several patterns that appear to be associated with higher churn rates. These findings can help the company focus its retention efforts on the customers who are most likely to leave.

---

### Key Findings

#### 1. Customers Who Have Not Ordered Recently Are More Likely to Churn

The strongest pattern observed in the analysis was related to recency. Customers who churned generally had a much longer gap since their last purchase compared to customers who stayed active.

This suggests that customers who have not placed an order for a long period should be monitored more closely and contacted before they become inactive permanently.

**Suggested Action:** Create a list of customers whose last purchase was more than 60 days ago and include them in retention activities.

---

#### 2. Higher Support Ticket Activity Is Linked to Higher Churn

Customers with multiple support tickets showed a higher churn rate than customers with few or no tickets. Ticket categories such as damaged products, product reactions and refund delays appeared more frequently among customers who later churned.

This indicates that service-related issues may be contributing to customer dissatisfaction.

**Suggested Action:** Review unresolved complaints and improve response times for customers who have raised repeated support requests.

---

#### 3. Loyalty Programme Members Tend to Churn Less

More than half of the customers in the dataset were not enrolled in the loyalty programme. The analysis showed that customers without a loyalty membership had a higher churn rate than customers in Silver, Gold and Platinum tiers.

While this does not prove that the loyalty programme directly reduces churn, it does suggest a relationship between loyalty membership and customer retention.

**Suggested Action:** Encourage eligible customers to join the loyalty programme and evaluate the results over time.

---

#### 4. Low Digital Engagement May Be a Warning Sign

Customers with no recent website or app activity were more likely to churn than customers who remained active online.

A lack of digital engagement may indicate declining interest in the brand and can serve as an early warning signal before churn occurs.

**Suggested Action:** Develop re-engagement campaigns specifically for customers who have not visited the website or app recently.

---

#### 5. Previous Campaigns Did Not Produce Consistent Results

The analysis showed differences in churn rates across campaign groups. In some cases, customers who received campaigns still churned at relatively high rates.

This suggests that campaign effectiveness may depend on customer characteristics and targeting strategy rather than simply sending more promotions.

**Suggested Action:** Review previous campaign performance and identify which customer segments responded most positively.

---

### Questions for Further Investigation

| Question                                                    | Reason                                                                  |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Is customer contact information up to date?                 | Retention campaigns will be ineffective if customers cannot be reached. |
| Are there unresolved support issues that require attention? | Customer dissatisfaction may continue if complaints remain unresolved.  |
| What budget is available for retention activities?          | Budget limitations may affect campaign design and targeting.            |
| Can loyalty programme invitations be automated?             | Automation would make large-scale retention efforts easier to manage.   |
| Which previous campaigns generated the best results?        | Understanding past performance can help improve future campaigns.       |

---

### Conclusion

The analysis indicates that churn is more common among customers who have not purchased recently, have little or no digital engagement, are not enrolled in the loyalty programme, or have experienced support-related issues.

Rather than targeting all customers with the same retention campaign, a more focused approach should be used. Giving priority to customers who show multiple risk signals is likely to make retention efforts more effective and help use marketing resources more efficiently.
